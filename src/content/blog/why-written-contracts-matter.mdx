It's tempting to rely on a handshake or a friendly understanding for smaller agreements. In practice, written contracts protect both sides even when trust is high.

## Memory fades, and interpretations differ

Two people can walk away from the same conversation with different understandings of what was agreed. A written contract removes the guesswork.

## Clear terms prevent disputes before they start

Specifying payment terms, timelines, and responsibilities in writing gives both sides something concrete to refer back to if questions arise later.

## A short agreement is still worth having

A contract does not need to be lengthy to be useful. Even a simple written summary of the key terms, signed by both parties, is far more protective than no documentation at all.

## Takeaway

Whether the agreement involves a service, a sale, or a partnership, taking a few extra minutes to put the terms in writing is a small effort that can prevent significant problems later.
