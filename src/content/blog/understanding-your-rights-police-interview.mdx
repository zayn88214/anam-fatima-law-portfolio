Being asked to speak with police can feel intimidating, even for people who have done nothing wrong. Knowing the basic shape of your rights can make the experience far less stressful.

## You have the right to remain silent

In most jurisdictions, you are not required to answer questions beyond confirming basic identifying information. Politely stating that you would like to speak with a lawyer before answering further questions is a reasonable and common response.

## Requests should be clear, not confrontational

Clarity matters more than tone. A calm, direct statement — such as asking whether you are free to leave, or requesting legal representation — is more useful than an emotional response.

## Write down what you remember afterward

As soon as possible after any interaction, write down what was said, by whom, and roughly when. These notes can be useful if the matter continues.

## This is general information, not legal advice

Rules vary by jurisdiction and specific circumstances change the analysis. If you are facing an active legal matter, seek guidance from a qualified professional as early as possible.
