# Tax Registration Basics
## Prepare before using IRIS
The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) uses IRIS for income-tax registration and returns. Requirements differ for individuals, associations, and companies.
- Gather CNIC, mobile number, email, address, and bank-account details.
- Keep business name, premises evidence, utility bill, and principal activity ready where applicable.
- Use only an official FBR domain and protect your login credentials.

# Individual registration
## Typical information
FBR’s registration guidance asks individuals for identity, contact, residence, and financial information. The exact fields and attachments can change.
- Confirm your CNIC and mobile ownership before starting.
- Record the registration/NTN reference and download the acknowledgement.
- Update changes promptly rather than opening duplicate profiles.

# Business records
## Keep the tax file current
Businesses may need partnership, incorporation, premises, bank, and representative information in addition to individual details.
- Keep invoices, expense proofs, payroll, withholding records, and sales-tax documents organised.
- Separate personal and business transactions where possible.
- Calendar return, payment, withholding, and registration-update dates.
---PAGE---
# If something goes wrong
## Verify before paying
Use FBR’s official helpline and portal notices for account or filing issues. Never share an OTP or password with an intermediary.
- Screenshot error messages and note the date and IRIS transaction ID.
- Reconcile tax payments with the CPR or bank confirmation.
- Seek qualified tax advice for penalties, audits, notices, or cross-border income.
