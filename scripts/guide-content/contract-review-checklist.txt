# Contract Review Checklist
## Before you sign
A contract can allocate money, risk, deadlines, and ownership. Read every page and identify the law and forum that apply. The Contract Act, 1872 remains a core reference, subject to later legislation.
- Confirm every party’s legal name, authority, address, and identity.
- Check that schedules, specifications, prices, and referenced policies are attached.
- Never sign a blank page or rely on a promise that is not written.

# Commercial terms
## Make the deal measurable
Ambiguous price, scope, delivery, acceptance, and payment terms create disputes.
- Define deliverables, milestones, taxes, currency, invoices, and late-payment consequences.
- State who bears shipping, insurance, approvals, and change-order costs.
- Include a process for variations, notices, and recordkeeping.
---PAGE---
# Risk and remedies
## Plan for problems
Review warranties, indemnities, liability caps, insurance, confidentiality, and intellectual-property ownership.
- Check whether exclusions are mutual and whether a cap has sensible exceptions.
- Identify termination triggers, cure periods, return of property, and survival clauses.
- Ensure dispute escalation, jurisdiction, and governing law are workable.
---PAGE---
# Signing and performance
## Keep an audit trail
Authority and execution formalities matter. Some transactions require witnesses, registration, stamping, or notarisation.
- Confirm the signatory’s board resolution, power of attorney, or other authority.
- Keep the signed version, amendments, invoices, approvals, and delivery evidence together.
- Calendar renewal, notice, warranty, and limitation dates.
