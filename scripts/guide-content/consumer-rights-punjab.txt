# Consumer Rights in Punjab
## Start with the transaction file
The Punjab Consumer Protection Act, 2005 provides a framework for consumer claims in Punjab. Keep the facts and documents together before approaching a forum.
- Save the receipt, invoice, warranty, order page, and payment confirmation.
- Write down the purchase date, seller details, defect, and requested remedy.
- Stop using unsafe goods and preserve their condition where possible.
---PAGE---
# Defective goods and services
## Describe the failure precisely
A complaint is stronger when it explains what was promised, what was supplied, and how it fell short.
- Photograph serial numbers, packaging, damage, and labels.
- Keep repair tickets, inspection reports, and all customer-service reference numbers.
- Separate direct loss from inconvenience and explain each amount.
---PAGE---
# Notice and resolution
## Give the seller a fair chance
Use a traceable written complaint and ask for a specific remedy, such as repair, replacement, refund, or correction.
- Include a reasonable response date and copies, not irreplaceable originals.
- Do not accept a settlement without reading its release and payment terms.
- Keep a call log and follow up in writing.
---PAGE---
# Filing preparation
## Organise the claim
Forum, limitation, fees, and required notice depend on the current law and the nature of the transaction.
- Prepare a one-page chronology and indexed document bundle.
- Identify the correct seller, manufacturer, service provider, and address for notice.
- Check the Punjab consumer protection portal or local legal-aid options for the current procedure.
